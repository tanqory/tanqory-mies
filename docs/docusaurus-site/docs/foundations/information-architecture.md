# Information Architecture

A clear **information architecture (IA)** is the backbone of every Tanqory product.  
Whether you design screens, write content, or build features, you're already doing IA work.  
This guide keeps IA decisions simple, scalable, and consistent.

## What Is IA
Information architecture is the practice of **organizing, structuring, and labeling content** so that people can find and use it easily and sustainably—across web, mobile, and future Tanqory surfaces.

## Why It Matters
- **Simplify complexity** – Make content easy to navigate and maintain.  
- **Support growth** – Good IA scales as products evolve without major rework.  
- **Create familiarity** – Merchants should feel at home across all Tanqory apps and devices.

## Core Principles

### 1. Show Users Where They Are
Good navigation enables effortless wayfinding.

- Provide multiple navigation layers: main nav, local nav, breadcrumbs  
- Use task-based navigation for common workflows  
- Offer secondary support like contextual links and search

### 2. One Home, Many Doors
Content should have a **single source of truth** but be reachable from different contexts and devices.

- Keep each piece of content in one location  
- Link to it wherever users need extra context

### 3. Avoid Overload
Give all necessary information—no more, no less.

- Use **progressive disclosure**: reveal details as users request them  
- Provide multiple access points instead of duplicating content  
- Remove redundant or outdated pages

### 4. Plan for Growth
IA is never final. Design it to evolve.

- Communicate early with other teams to avoid conflicts  
- Create flexible content groups that can expand or shrink  
- Ensure patterns adapt to web, iOS, Android, and new touchpoints

## Practical Tips
- **Audit regularly** – Review navigation and links as features change.  
- **Test with real users** – Validate that people can find what they need in under three clicks.  
- **Document decisions** – Keep a short record of why each IA choice was made.  
- **Think cross-platform** – Consider desktop, mobile, chatbots, POS, and future Tanqory channels.

## Quick Checklist
Before shipping a new page or feature, confirm:
- Navigation clearly shows the current location.  
- Content exists in one place with clear links elsewhere.  
- Information density feels balanced—no unnecessary details.  
- Structure can scale without breaking when more markets or features are added.

A strong IA makes Tanqory products **faster to use, easier to maintain, and ready for global growth**.
