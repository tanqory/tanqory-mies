# Naming & Labels

Consistent naming creates predictable, learnable interfaces. Users rely on familiar patterns to navigate confidently.

## Naming Principles

### Use Familiar Language
- **Common terms over clever ones** - "Settings" not "Preferences"
- **User's mental model** - "Shopping cart" not "Order container"
- **Industry standards** - Follow established conventions

### Be Specific and Clear
- **Descriptive over generic** - "Add payment method" not "Add item"
- **Action-oriented** - "Save changes" not "Save"
- **Avoid ambiguity** - "Delete account" not "Remove"

### Maintain Consistency
- **Same concept, same word** - Don't vary between "user" and "member"
- **Parallel structure** - "Create", "Edit", "Delete" (not "Create", "Modify", "Remove")
- **Cross-platform alignment** - Web and mobile use identical terms

## Interface Element Labels

### Buttons
**Make actions clear and specific**

✅ **Good examples:**
- "Create account"
- "Add to cart"
- "Send message"
- "Download file"

❌ **Avoid these:**
- "Submit" (submit what?)
- "OK" (OK to what?)
- "Click here" (not descriptive)

### Form Fields
**Help users understand what to enter**

✅ **Clear labels:**
- "Email address"
- "Password (8+ characters)"
- "Phone number"
- "Company name (optional)"

❌ **Unclear labels:**
- "Login" (username? email?)
- "Name" (first? full? company?)
- "Info" (what kind of info?)

### Navigation
**Guide users to their destination**

✅ **Descriptive navigation:**
- "Account settings"
- "Order history"
- "Help center"
- "Contact support"

❌ **Vague navigation:**
- "Tools"
- "More"
- "Other"

## Content Labels

### Page Titles
- **Describe the page content** - "Payment methods" not "Methods"
- **Unique within the app** - Avoid duplicate titles
- **Scannable hierarchy** - Clear relationship to parent pages

### Section Headings
- **Parallel structure** - All headings use same grammatical pattern
- **Logical grouping** - Related content under descriptive headings
- **Progressive disclosure** - Most important information first

### Status and State Labels
- **Clear current state** - "Draft", "Published", "Archived"
- **Progress indicators** - "Step 2 of 4", "3 items remaining"
- **System feedback** - "Saving...", "Sent", "Failed"

## Naming Conventions

### Text Case Rules
- **Sentence case for most UI** - "Add new contact"
- **Title case for proper nouns** - "Google Drive Integration"
- **ALL CAPS sparingly** - Only for emphasis or system alerts

### Abbreviations and Acronyms
- **Spell out on first use** - "Customer Relationship Management (CRM)"
- **Use familiar abbreviations** - "FAQ", "URL", "PDF"
- **Avoid internal jargon** - Users shouldn't need to decode meanings

### Numbers and Symbols
- **Spell out one through nine** - "Three options" but "10 results"
- **Use symbols consistently** - Always "&" or always "and"
- **Clear mathematical operations** - "×" for close, "+" for add

## Special Contexts

### Error and Success Messages
- **Outcome-focused labels** - "Payment successful" not "Success"
- **Next action clear** - "Try again" not "Retry"
- **Specific problem identification** - "Invalid email format" not "Error"

### Mobile Considerations
- **Shorter labels** - Space constraints require concision
- **Touch-friendly actions** - "Tap to call" not "Click to call"
- **Context-aware labeling** - Consider where users are when they see the label

### Accessibility
- **Screen reader friendly** - Labels work when read aloud
- **No reliance on color/icons alone** - Text accompanies visual cues
- **Descriptive link text** - "View full pricing details" not "Learn more"

## Label Testing

### Validation Methods
- **Card sorting** - Do users group items as expected?
- **First-click testing** - Do users click the right thing?
- **Tree testing** - Can users find information using your labels?

### Common Problems
- **Ambiguous terms** - Words that mean different things to different users
- **Technical jargon** - Internal language that users don't understand
- **Inconsistent patterns** - Same action labeled differently across screens

## Global Considerations

### Translation-Friendly Labels
- **Simple sentence structure** - Easier to translate accurately
- **Cultural neutrality** - Avoid region-specific references
- **Context provision** - Help translators understand usage

### Length Variations
- **Plan for expansion** - Some languages require 30-50% more space
- **Flexible layouts** - Labels should work at various lengths
- **Alternative phrasings** - Shorter versions for constrained spaces

Clear, consistent naming reduces cognitive load and builds user confidence throughout the entire experience.
