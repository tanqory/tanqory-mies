# Writing Basics

Clear, concise writing forms the foundation of excellent user experiences. These principles guide all content creation.

## Core Principles

### Write for Your Audience
- **Know your users** - Understand their goals, context, and technical level
- **Match their mental model** - Use familiar terms and concepts
- **Respect their time** - Get to the point quickly

### Be Clear and Direct
- **Use simple language** - Choose everyday words over technical jargon
- **Write short sentences** - Aim for 20 words or fewer
- **Active voice preferred** - "Click Save" not "Save should be clicked"

### Structure for Scanning
- **Front-load important information** - Lead with what matters most
- **Use descriptive headings** - Help users navigate quickly
- **Break up long text** - Lists, bullets, and white space improve readability

## Voice and Tone

### Tanqory Mies Voice
- **Helpful and direct** - Focus on user success
- **Confident but humble** - We know our stuff, but we're here to help
- **Consistent across platforms** - Same personality everywhere

### Tone Adaptation
- **Error states**: Apologetic but solution-focused
- **Success states**: Encouraging without celebration
- **Instructions**: Clear and supportive
- **Marketing**: Honest about benefits

## Writing Process

### Before Writing
1. **Define the user goal** - What are they trying to accomplish?
2. **Identify key information** - What do they need to know?
3. **Choose the right format** - Button, paragraph, list, or table?

### While Writing
1. **Start with a clear headline** - Summarize the main point
2. **Support with details** - Add context without overwhelming
3. **Include next steps** - What can the user do now?

### After Writing
1. **Read aloud** - Does it sound natural?
2. **Test with users** - Do they understand and act correctly?
3. **Revise based on feedback** - Content improves through iteration

## Common Patterns

### Button Labels
- Use action verbs: "Save Changes", "Add Item", "Delete Account"
- Be specific: "Send Email" not just "Send"

### Form Labels
- Clear and descriptive: "Email Address" not just "Email"
- Indicate requirements: "Password (8+ characters)"

### Help Text
- Explain the why: "We use this to send order confirmations"
- Provide examples: "Enter your city (e.g., San Francisco)"

Good writing disappears - users accomplish their goals without noticing the words that helped them get there.
