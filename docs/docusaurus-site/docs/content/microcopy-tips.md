# Microcopy Tips

Small words make big differences. Strategic microcopy guides users through experiences and prevents confusion.

## What is Microcopy?

### The Small Text That Matters
- **Button labels** - What happens when users click?
- **Form helpers** - Guidance for input fields
- **Error messages** - When things go wrong
- **Empty states** - What users see when there's no content
- **Loading messages** - Feedback during wait times

### Why It Matters
- **Reduces support tickets** - Clear microcopy prevents user confusion
- **Improves conversion** - Users complete tasks when they understand next steps
- **Builds trust** - Helpful, accurate text creates confidence

## Button Microcopy

### Action-Oriented Labels
**Make every button clearly communicate its action**

✅ **Specific actions:**
- "Add to cart" → User knows item goes to shopping cart
- "Send message" → Clear that message will be delivered
- "Download report" → File will be downloaded
- "Create account" → New account will be made

❌ **Vague actions:**
- "Submit" → Submit what? Where does it go?
- "Continue" → Continue to where?
- "OK" → OK for what action?
- "Done" → What's finished?

### Loading States
**Tell users what's happening during waits**

✅ **Informative loading:**
- "Creating your account..."
- "Uploading file..."
- "Processing payment..."
- "Saving changes..."

## Form Microcopy

### Input Field Helpers
**Guide users to successful completion**

✅ **Helpful guidance:**
- "We'll use this to send order confirmations" (for email field)
- "Choose something you'll remember" (for password)
- "This appears on your public profile" (for display name)

### Placeholder Examples
**Show format without being prescriptive**

✅ **Good placeholders:**
- "yourname@company.com" (email format)
- "(555) 123-4567" (phone format)
- "San Francisco, CA" (location format)

❌ **Avoid these:**
- Actual instructions in placeholder text
- Placeholder text that disappears when typing starts
- Examples that become form validation

## Error and Success Microcopy

### Error Prevention
**Help users avoid mistakes before they happen**

✅ **Proactive guidance:**
- "Password must include 8+ characters" (before they type)
- "File must be less than 10MB" (near upload button)
- "Changes save automatically" (reduces anxiety about data loss)

### Recovery Messaging
**When errors happen, provide clear next steps**

✅ **Solution-focused:**
- "Email address not found. Check spelling or create new account."
- "File too large. Compress or choose different file."
- "Connection lost. Check internet and try again."

## Empty State Microcopy

### First-Time Experience
**Help users understand what to do when starting**

✅ **Encouraging empty states:**
- "No projects yet. Create your first project to get started."
- "Your inbox is empty. New messages will appear here."
- "No team members added. Invite colleagues to collaborate."

### Context-Aware Messaging
**Different messages for different situations**

- **No search results:** "No matches found. Try different keywords."
- **No data yet:** "Data will appear here once you connect your account."
- **Completed items:** "All done! Completed items appear here."

Good microcopy feels invisible when it works—users accomplish their goals without noticing the words that guided them there.
