# Inclusive Language

Language shapes how people feel about your product. Inclusive writing creates welcoming experiences for diverse audiences.

## Inclusive Principles

### Write for Everyone
- **Assume diverse audiences** - Your users have different backgrounds, abilities, and experiences
- **Avoid assumptions** - Don't assume technical knowledge, family structures, or cultural contexts
- **Choose accessible language** - Simple words work better for global and diverse audiences

### Be Respectful and Neutral
- **Person-first language** - "Person with disability" not "disabled person"
- **Avoid stereotypes** - Don't assume roles, relationships, or characteristics
- **Stay professional** - Friendly but respectful tone across all content

## Language Patterns

### Gender-Inclusive Writing
**Use neutral alternatives when gender isn't relevant**

✅ **Inclusive alternatives:**
- "Team members" instead of "guys"
- "Everyone" instead of "guys and girls"
- "Staff" or "people" instead of "manpower"
- "They/them" for individuals when appropriate

❌ **Avoid assumptions:**
- "When a user logs in, he sees..."
- "Every developer needs his tools..."
- "The admin and his team..."

### Ability-Inclusive Language
**Avoid ableist language and metaphors**

✅ **Better alternatives:**
- "Hidden" or "not visible" instead of "blind to"
- "Blocked" or "prevented" instead of "crippled"
- "Ineffective" instead of "lame"
- "Unresponsive" instead of "deaf to"

### Age and Experience Inclusive
**Don't assume age or technical expertise**

✅ **Inclusive approach:**
- "New to this?" instead of "Even grandma can use it"
- "Getting started" instead of "Dummy's guide"
- "Learn more" instead of "Obviously..."

## Cultural Sensitivity

### Global Considerations
- **Avoid cultural idioms** - "It's a piece of cake" may not translate well
- **Use universal examples** - Names, locations, and references from various cultures
- **Consider religious neutrality** - "Holiday break" instead of assuming specific holidays

### Socioeconomic Awareness
- **Don't assume resources** - Not everyone has high-speed internet or latest devices
- **Avoid lifestyle assumptions** - Different work schedules, living situations, family structures
- **Include various scenarios** - Single parents, caregivers, different economic situations

## Writing Techniques

### Neutral Defaults
**Make inclusive choices your standard approach**

- **Names in examples** - Use diverse names from various cultures
- **Relationship terms** - "Partner" when marital status unknown
- **Family structures** - Include different family compositions
- **Professional roles** - Avoid gender assumptions in job titles

### Avoiding Exclusionary Language
**Words and phrases that might alienate users**

❌ **Exclusionary patterns:**
- "Obviously" (implies everyone should know)
- "Just" (minimizes difficulty)
- "Everyone knows" (assumes universal knowledge)
- "Normal users" (implies others are abnormal)

✅ **Inclusive alternatives:**
- "To clarify" instead of "obviously"
- "You can" instead of "just do this"
- "Many people" instead of "everyone"
- "Most users" instead of "normal users"

## Accessibility in Language

### Clear Communication
- **Plain language first** - Choose simple words when possible
- **Define technical terms** - Explain jargon and acronyms
- **Short sentences** - Easier to understand and translate
- **Active voice** - More direct and clear

### Cognitive Accessibility
- **Predictable patterns** - Consistent language helps users navigate
- **Clear instructions** - Step-by-step without assumptions
- **Error prevention** - Helpful guidance before mistakes occur

## Testing and Review

### Review Process
- **Diverse perspectives** - Have people from different backgrounds review content
- **Read aloud** - Does it sound natural and respectful?
- **Global check** - Would this work for international audiences?

### Red Flags to Watch
- **Generational references** - Pop culture that dates content
- **Regional assumptions** - Geography, weather, local customs
- **Economic assumptions** - Spending habits, lifestyle choices
- **Technology assumptions** - Device types, internet speeds, technical skills

## Implementation Guidelines

### Systematic Approach
1. **Audit existing content** - Identify potentially exclusionary language
2. **Create alternatives list** - Document preferred inclusive terms
3. **Team training** - Share guidelines with all content creators
4. **Regular review** - Language evolves, so should your guidelines

### Getting Feedback
- **User research** - Ask diverse users about your content
- **Community input** - Engage with user groups and communities
- **Accessibility experts** - Get professional guidance when needed

Inclusive language isn't about political correctness—it's about creating products that work well for everyone and make all users feel welcome and valued.
